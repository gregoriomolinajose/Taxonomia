## What Went Well
- **Separation of Concerns**: Building `Engine_Graph.js` entirely isolated from `Engine_DB.js` created a mathematical boundary. `Engine_DB` handles atomic saving and Data Models, while `Engine_Graph` is ignorant of persistence and only performs memory traversal (Topological assertions).
- **Test-Driven Design**: Writing `__tests__/Engine_Graph_Topology.test.js` proved to be the bedrock of the entire Epic E8. We captured DAG infractions deterministically before wiring it into the production runtime.

## What to Improve
- We initially implemented Max Depth and Cycles as BFS iterative arrays (`queue.push`) which structurally prevented us from solving Poly-Trees (M:N) with shared cousin branches. The lack of algorithmic foresight here forced us into an S8.3 Fast-Follow sequence to convert it to a DFS Memoized recursion. 

## Heutagogical Checkpoint
1. **What did you learn?** 
   - I learned that in serverless environments, simulating database topological edges in active memory must be strictly agnostic of the datastore shape (e.g. converting `id_nodo_padre` keys to Hash Maps mapping `[childId] = [...parents]`) to avoid mapping arrays repetitively in $O(N^2)$.
2. **What would you change about the process?** 
   - I should heavily cross-examine Big-O complexities of traversal functions mathematically before committing the first DAG blocker to `develop`.
3. **Are there improvements for the framework?**
   - None at this stage.
4. **What are you more capable of now?**
   - My competence in intercepting transactions right before their commit (Pre-Merge Hooks) inside custom `orchestrateNestedSave` pipelines is robust.
